BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs at rest. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) includes physical activity and gives a better estimate of your daily calorie needs.

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ [height (m)]²
BMI = (weight (lb) ÷ [height (in)]²) × 703

Mifflin-St Jeor (when body fat is unknown):
Men: (10 × weight) + (6.25 × height) − (5 × age) + 5
Women: (10 × weight) + (6.25 × height) − (5 × age) − 161
Katch-McArdle (when body fat % is known):
BMR = 370 + (21.6 × Lean Body Mass)
Lean Body Mass = weight × (1 − bodyFat% ÷ 100)
